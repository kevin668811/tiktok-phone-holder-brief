Key Selling Points

First: It looks “wrong” The slanted shape looks unlike a normal band at first glance, creating curiosity, stops, and comments.
Second: The angle has a purpose While exercising, cycling, or driving, you can glance at the time without deliberately turning your wrist.
Left-wrist wearing angle This band is designed for left-wrist wear only. The slanted angle makes it easier to glance at the time while exercising, cycling, or driving.
Secondary Benefits
Soft silicone material Lightweight and comfortable for everyday wear, commuting, and workouts.
Breathable perforations Helps reduce stuffiness during exercise and gives the band a lighter, sportier look.
Secure buckle design Less likely to loosen during wear or activity, making it suitable for running, cycling, and gym content.

How to film the first 3 seconds

Do not overexplain. Use the visual to make viewers wonder whether the band was installed wrong.

1. Make it look installed wrong Open directly on a close-up of the slanted band. Do not explain it yet.
2. Show it on the wrist Put the band on and prove that it is not broken or installed incorrectly.
3. Check the time while cycling Show how the time is visible while cycling or driving without turning the wrist.
