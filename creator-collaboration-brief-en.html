Do not just explain the product. In the first 5 seconds, show that it solves phone, laptop, tablet, travel, and charging scenarios.

Main Selling Points

Keep the voiceover natural, like a real useful find. Show what it does right away instead of overexplaining specs.

Flexible Angles The bendable body quickly turns into a desk stand or a screen-side hanging stand.
Phone And Tablet Use Do not only show a phone. Tablet shots make the product feel more useful and higher value.
Charging Opening The bottom has space for a charging cable, so the phone can stay on the stand while charging.
Foldable And Portable It folds down for pockets or bags, making it easy to carry to work, travel, or study spaces.

Show The Result First

The first shot should immediately show the phone mounted beside a laptop or on a desk, so viewers understand the use case right away.

Opening Visuals

For the first 5 seconds, choose one or two different scenes. Keep the visuals faster than the voiceover.

Phone Stand

Show the phone standing on the desk first, so viewers instantly see the result.

Laptop Side Mount

Show the phone beside the laptop to hit the desk-work pain point.

Tablet Stand

Cut to a tablet setup to make the product feel more versatile.

Travel Carry

Show it folded and placed into a pocket or bag.

Cup Pad

Show the folded stand temporarily supporting a cup or small desk item.

Opening Hooks

Pick one hook only. Do not read all three. The visuals should move faster than the voiceover.

Pain Point

Stop leaning your phone against random cups, keyboards, or the side of your laptop.

Best visual: the phone mounted steadily beside the laptop.
Surprise

Best visual: quick cuts through three different use cases.
Useful Find

Basic Version Vs. Upgraded Version.

The upgraded version builds on the original stand, hang, and fold design, with a more practical bottom charging opening. Viewers can instantly understand the upgrade: the phone can stay on the stand while watching, scrolling, and charging.

  • Charging opening: the phone can stay upright while plugged in.
  • Stronger support impression: better for phone and tablet demonstration shots.
  • More scenes to film: desk, laptop side, bedside, travel, and storage.
  • Clearer upgrade story: the comparison makes the new version feel more worth buying.

Shooting Notes

Must Shoot
  • Fast scene cuts in the first 5 seconds.
  • Hands bending the stand.
  • Phone mounted beside a laptop.
  • Tablet support shot.
  • Close-up of charging while the phone is on the stand.
Avoid
  • Starting with packaging or a slow unboxing.
  • Only saying the product name in the first few seconds.
  • Absolute claims like "never falls" or "100% non-slip."
  • Medical claims such as fixing posture or neck pain.
  • Tablet shots that look shaky or unstable.
